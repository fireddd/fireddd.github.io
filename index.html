Shubhransh Jagota

ai systems · payments & sdks · machine learning · quant

I'm a software engineer based in India, and a polyglot by habit — I go where the problem is. I've built payments infrastructure and the SDKs on top of it, designed AI systems and LLM agents, done machine learning and data science at IBM, and worked as a quant at Morgan Stanley, AlphaGrep and WorldQuant.

Most recently I was at Zamp.ai, where I designed a natural language–controlled browser agent that autonomously carries out multi-step web tasks. I also led the migration of the Actions Hub to a modular architecture, decoupling action registration from agent execution, and instrumented our LLM systems with Langfuse.

Before that I spent nearly three years at PhonePe on payments infrastructure. I led the Java SDK for their B2B payment gateway — the SDK family carried tens of crores in total payment volume — owned the Epoch scheduling framework, and ran disaster recovery for online merchant services.

I came into engineering through quant. I built valuation adjustments on Morgan Stanley's fixed income desk, wrote alphas at WorldQuant and AlphaGrep, and before that built reinforcement learning environments as a visiting researcher at the MIT Media Lab. I studied Computer Science and Economics at BITS Pilani.
