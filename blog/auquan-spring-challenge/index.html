Auquan · Spring Challenge · 2019

Auquan Spring Challenge

Bored one week, I entered a competition. A plain tuned model beat everything else I tried.

  • Python
  • Machine learning
  • Logistic regression
  • Hyperparameter tuning
  • Winner

One week in 2019 I was getting bored. I saw a competition — Auquan's Spring Challenge — and decided to spend some time on it. There wasn't much more to the decision than that. I entered, submitted something, and then went back to whatever else I was doing.

What I actually did

I was working through ML courses at the time, and I wanted to see whether any of what was being taught had a practical application. So I treated the challenge as a place to try things, without worrying much about where I'd place.

I tried a fair amount. In the end, what performed best was logistic regression with hyperparameter tuning — not the most exotic thing I reached for, just the thing that worked.

I more or less forgot about it after submitting. The judging took a while.

The email

Then, months later, an email arrived. I was one of the winning competitors. My submission had scored above 0.03, which the judges had decided put me in the top prize bracket — and there was a cash prize attached to it.

The point

I got lucky in the end. But I only got lucky because I entered, and I very nearly didn't — it was a bored week and it would have been easier to do nothing.

You miss every shot you don't take.
